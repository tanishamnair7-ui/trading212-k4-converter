üí∞ Swedish Capital Gains Tax Basics

Tax Rate

Capital gains from stocks and ETFs are taxed at 30%.

How It Works

  • ‚úì Profits from stock sales = Capital Gains
  • ‚úì Losses can be deducted from gains in the same year
  • ‚úì Net result is taxed at 30%
  • ‚úì Losses exceeding gains can offset other capital income

Example

Your trades:

  • Total gains: 10,000 SEK
  • Total losses: -1,000 SEK
  • Net result: 9,000 SEK

Tax calculation:

9,000 SEK √ó 30% = 2,700 SEK tax

üîß Troubleshooting

"No sell transactions found"

Solutions:

  • Check you exported the correct date range
  • Make sure the file is from Trading 212
  • If you only bought stocks (no sells), you don't need K4

Total doesn't match my calculation

This is normal:

  • Trading 212 uses the average cost method
  • Currency conversion timing may differ
  • Broker fees are included

Solution: Use Trading 212's reported P/L - it's official and correct.

I have trades from multiple years

Solution: Export and process each year separately. File separate K4 forms for each tax year.
