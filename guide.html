üí∞ Understanding Swedish Capital Gains Tax

Tax Rate

Capital gains from stocks and ETFs are taxed at 30% in Sweden.

How It Works

  • Profits from stock sales = Capital Gains
  • Losses can be deducted from gains in the same year
  • Net result is taxed at 30%
  • Losses exceeding gains can offset other capital income

Example Calculation

Your trades:

  • Total gains: 10,000 SEK
  • Total losses: 1,000 SEK
  • Net result: 9,000 SEK

Tax calculation:

9,000 SEK √ó 30% = 2,700 SEK tax

üîç Troubleshooting

Error: "No sell transactions found"

Solution:

  • Make sure you exported the correct date range
  • If you only made buy transactions, there's nothing to report on K4
  • Check that the CSV file is from Trading 212 (not another broker)

Total doesn't match my calculation

Possible reasons:

  • Trading 212 uses the average cost method (genomsnittsmetoden)
  • Currency conversion timing may differ slightly
  • Broker fees are included in the result

Solution: Use the broker's reported P/L (Result column) - it's official and what Skatteverket expects.

I have trades from multiple years

Solution: Export and process each year separately. File separate K4 forms for each tax year.

What about dividends?

Note: Dividends are NOT reported on K4. They go on a different form (Ku10 or automatically from bank). This tool only handles capital gains/losses from selling securities.
